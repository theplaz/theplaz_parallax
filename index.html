  • As an American, if you are no longer able to work, you can apply for Social Security Disability Insurance, and if approved, earn an average of $1,100 a month. Your detailed application is reviewed about one of the 5-10,000 state disability adjudicators using antiquated 1970s era “green-screen” software. Social Security tried to centralize and modernize the system, but spent 7 years and $300 million to accomplish nothing - before deciding they wanted to start over Silicon Valley-style - working with the United States Digital Service, a division of the White House set up by President Obama’s after the HealthCare.gov debacle.

    For this project, I was the lead Product Manager from USDS. I worked with a team of agency employees, designers, engineers, and a small team of end users to design the core of the application which could grow over time. I established the Product process from scratch, taught the government employees Product Management, and set a high standard for the team.

    We built an initial MVP in far less time and with far less people than the previous project. An independent audit later found that all alpha users surveyed were satisfied with new system: its ease of use, its power/speed, and their involvement in design process.

  • Cards Prototype

    We are getting more and more notifications on our phones. What if we used the power of machine learning to identify which notifications were actually important?

    I proposed a multi-platform “Cards Platform” to create a perfect balance of your notifications, contextual information, email, and the news in a single, engaging cards stream. Could we build a product experience that was as engaging as Facebook but around your personal information and the news? I prepared pitches, mocks, and product architecture plans for card submission, card ranking, and card serving/display.

    Search in Aviate

    What if you could have a single search box on your Android homescreen which provided a perfect blend of device and web results - so you didn’t need to think about which app to use for a particular search. Spotlight on iOS is device focused and Google for Android is web search focused - could there be a combined search interface which works for both? Could we serve an integrated result set - while using a webview to cut development time? Could such a product hook Aviate users with device search, and then have them build a habit around using Yahoo web search?

    I launched the first version of Search in Aviate - the main acquisition thesis behind Aviate - in 3 months. Rather than launch a simple search box, I looked at what made Aviate successful and for gaps in competitor’s products. I worked with design to perfect every detail of the interaction; and got design approval with minimal changes. I worked with the tech team to deliver web results in webview - shaving a year of development off the project, while still providing a cohesive experience. I worked with marketing to position the product and analyzed the data post launch to suggest future changes.

    Aviate Onboarding Optimization

    How can we change Aviate’s Onboarding experience to maximize retention? By getting the user in the product faster? By telling the user more about how Aviate works up front?

    By having the user customize the Aviate experience more? Through various small experiments, I was able to improve Aviate’s 3 day retention by about 10%.

    Mobile Search

    What if we took a mobile-first approach to search? Instead of providing 10 blue links, could we surface inspiring and informational content about the entity directly on the search result page?

    I was asked to join a new upstart team. I researched oppertunities around quick facts (travel, recipes, etc) and oppertunities to partner on the creation of card contents.

    Geo Platform

    What if Yahoo products could become more personalized by more accurately predicting your location through better using signals and data available to Yahoo?

    I completely rebuilt Yahoo’s location stack; simplified and redesigned the API, rebuilt the serving stack, and refreshed all the internal logic. I helped other Yahoo properties understand how to correctly implement the new API in their user experience. I helped increase consistency across the Yahoo network by moving more logic from properties into the platform. I worked with the engineering team to use machine learning to improve user current location accuracy from ~60% to 80%. I worked closely with legal and policy team to ensure compliance. Lastly, I also built a business case for location history and investigated venue identification strategies, which Yahoo implemented after I left.
